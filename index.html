– a2z family lab –
Family Research and Evidence-Based Family Coaching

Welcome! The a2z family lab was born from the curiosity and drive of two dedicated researchers, Ziwen Teuber and Anke Weber, psychologists with PhDs from Germany. Founded within the Department of Behavioral and Cognitive Sciences at the University of Luxembourg, our lab explores how children, adolescents, and families navigate life and learning. At a2z family Lab, research meets real life: every study uncovers insights that deepen our understanding of human development and family functioning as well as inform our family coaching programs to support parents and caregivers in fostering thriving, resilient families.

Ziwen Teuber, Dr. rer. nat.

Founder, Senior Researcher and Facilitator

My name is Ziwen Teuber. I am a Senior Researcher holding PhD supervision rights. I also lead the FNR CORE project "Unpacking Parental Involvement in Challenging Achievement Situations" (UPICAS).

My journey to Luxembourg began in Germany, where I earned my BSc and MSc degrees in Psychology between 2011 and 2016, and completed my PhD in Psychology at Bielefeld University in 2021. During my doctoral studies, I investigated school burnout among Chinese and German school students that sparked my broader interest in how young people navigate pressure, expectations, and emotional demands. Since then, my research has taken a more family-centered direction. I am particularly fascinated by how everyday parent–child interactions shape well-being and development, and how evidence-based tools can help families handle the challenges they encounter.

I draw on frameworks from multiple psychological subdisciplines and apply rigorous methodologies to address contemporary challenges in education, family life, and society. I have multi-sectoral experience and aim to use my knowledge in science, praxis, and political communication to impact research and society. I am currently co-editing the "Handbook of Educational Psychology" with Nadine Spörer (University of Potsdam) and Samuel Greiff (Technical University of Munich), to be published by Springer. The handbook is scheduled for release in autumn 2027.

Languages: English, German, and Mandarin

Anke Weber, Dr. phil.

Founder, Researcher

My name is Anke Weber. I am a Researcher. My research focuses on developmental and educational psychology, with a particular interest in family interactions and child development. I collaborate closely with international researchers and local practitioners to explore evidence-based interventions that enhance well-being and learning outcomes. My work combines rigorous research methodologies with practical applications, aiming to impact both science and society.

Languages: English, German, and French
